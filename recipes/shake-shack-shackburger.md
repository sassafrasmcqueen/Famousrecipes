# Shake Shack ShackBurger

The modern burger standard: a smashed beef patty, gooey American cheese, and the tangy, creamy ShackSauce on a toasted potato roll.

### Ingredients
**ShackSauce:**
- **Mayonnaise:** 120g (1/2 cup)
- **Dijon Mustard:** 15g (1 tbsp)
- **Ketchup:** 10g (3/4 tsp)
- **Kosher Dill Pickle Brine:** 1.25ml (1/4 tsp)
- **Cayenne Pepper:** A tiny pinch
- **Paprika:** A tiny pinch (for color)

**The Burger:**
- **Ground Beef (80/20):** 113g (4oz) - ideally a blend of sirloin, brisket, and chuck
- **American Cheese:** 1 slice
- **Martin’s Potato Roll:** 1 roll
- **Unsalted Butter:** 10g (melted, for the bun)
- **Plum Tomato:** 2 slices
- **Green Leaf Lettuce:** 1 leaf
- **Salt & Pepper:** To taste (coarse salt preferred)

### Instructions
1. **Make the ShackSauce:** In a small bowl, whisk together the mayo, Dijon, ketchup, pickle brine, cayenne, and paprika. Let it sit in the fridge for at least 30 minutes to develop flavor.
2. **Prep the Bun:** Slice the potato roll (not all the way through, keep a "hinge"). Brush the inside with melted butter and toast on a griddle until golden brown.
3. **Smash the Patty:** 
   - Roll the cold ground beef into a loose ball. 
   - Place the ball on a very hot, ungreased stainless steel or cast iron skillet.
   - Using a heavy spatula (and a second tool to press down), smash the patty until it is very thin and significantly wider than the bun.
   - Season generously with salt and pepper.
4. **The Sear:** Cook without moving for 2 minutes until a dark brown, lacy crust forms. 
5. **Flip and Cheese:** Scrape under the patty carefully to keep the crust intact. Flip, and immediately place the slice of American cheese on top. Cook for another 30-45 seconds.
6. **Assembly (Bottom to Top):**
   - Toasted Bottom Bun
   - Smashed Patty with Cheese
   - 2 Slices of Plum Tomato
   - Green Leaf Lettuce
   - Generous layer of ShackSauce on the top bun
   - Top Bun

### Secret Tips
- **The Smash:** You must smash the beef early (within the first 10 seconds of hitting the pan) to achieve the signature crispy edges without losing the juices.
- **Potato Rolls:** Martin's Potato Rolls are the non-negotiable choice for an authentic Shake Shack experience.
- **The "Hinge":** Keeping the bun connected at the back helps hold the sauce and juices in as you eat.

### Origin Story
Shake Shack started in 2001 as a humble hot dog cart in Madison Square Park, NYC, founded by Danny Meyer. The ShackBurger was introduced when it became a permanent stand in 2004. It was designed to be a high-end version of a classic roadside burger, focusing on premium beef blends.
