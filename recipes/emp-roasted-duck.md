# Eleven Madison Park's Roasted Duck with Lavender and Honey

Formerly the signature dish of NYC's Eleven Madison Park, this honey-lavender duck is legendary for its incredibly thin, glass-like skin and aromatic spice crust. It is a multi-day process that represents the pinnacle of high-end poultry preparation.

## Ingredients

### The Duck
- **1 Whole** Muscovy Duck (approx. 2.2kg - 2.5kg)
- **100g** Clover honey (high quality)
- **20g** Salt

### The Spice Crust
- **2 tbsp** Cumin seeds
- **2 tbsp** Coriander seeds
- **2 tbsp** Sichuan peppercorns
- **2 tbsp** Dried culinary lavender

### Duck Jus
- **500g** Duck bones and wings (chopped)
- **1** Carrot, diced
- **1** Onion, diced
- **1** Leek, white part only, diced
- **500ml** Chicken stock
- **100ml** Dry white wine

## Instructions

### 1. Air-Drying (The Secret to Crispy Skin)
1. Clean the duck and remove any excess fat.
2. Carefully separate the skin from the meat using your fingers or a dull spoon, being careful not to tear the skin. This allows air to circulate.
3. Truss the duck tightly.
4. Hang the duck in a refrigerator or place it on a wire rack over a tray. Let it air-dry for at least 3-5 days. In the restaurant, they dry it for up to 14 days. This process dehydrates the skin so it becomes thin and crisp.

### 2. Preparing the Spice Rub
1. Toast the cumin, coriander, and Sichuan peppercorns in a dry pan until fragrant.
2. Let the spices cool, then grind them coarsely in a spice grinder or mortar and pestle.
3. Stir in the dried lavender.

### 3. Glazing and Crusting
1. Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F).
2. Heat the honey in a small saucepan until liquid and easy to brush.
3. Brush the entire duck generously with honey.
4. Pack the ground spice mixture onto the honey-coated skin, covering it completely. The honey acts as the "glue" for the aromatic crust.

### 4. The Roast
1. Place the duck on a rack in a roasting pan.
2. Roast for approximately 15 minutes at high heat, then reduce the temperature to 160°C (325°F).
3. Continue roasting until the internal breast temperature reaches 52°C (125°F) for medium-rare.
4. **Crucial:** Rest the duck for at least 20-30 minutes before carving. This allows the juices to redistribute and the skin to set.

### 5. Duck Jus
1. Roast duck bones and trimmings until dark brown.
2. Add aromatics (carrot, onion, leek) and sauté until caramelized.
3. Deglaze with white wine and reduce until dry.
4. Add chicken stock and simmer for 1.5 hours.
5. Strain through a chinois and reduce to a syrupy consistency.

## Secret Tips
- **The Skin Separation:** Separating the skin from the meat is the professional technique that ensures the fat renders out completely, resulting in that "glass-like" texture.
- **Honey Quality:** Use a mild clover honey so it doesn't overpower the delicate lavender and spices.
- **Precision Temperature:** Use a digital probe thermometer. High-end duck is always served medium-rare to maintain its silkiness.

## Origin
Created by Chef **Daniel Humm** for **Eleven Madison Park** in New York City, this dish was the cornerstone of their 3-Michelin-star menu for over a decade. It became so iconic that it remained on the menu even as the restaurant's style evolved, until they transitioned to a fully plant-based menu in 2021.
