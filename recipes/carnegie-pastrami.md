# Carnegie Deli-Style Mile-High Pastrami Sandwich

The ultimate New York deli experience: tender, fatty, spice-rubbed pastrami stacked high on rye bread with a side of spicy brown mustard.

### Ingredients
**The Meat & Brine:**
- **Beef Navel (or Brisket):** 5-6 lbs (Navel is more authentic for its fat content).
- **Water:** 1 gallon.
- **Kosher Salt:** 150g.
- **Sugar:** 100g.
- **Pink Curing Salt (Prague Powder #1):** 25g.
- **Garlic:** 5 cloves, smashed.
- **Pickling Spice:** 3 tbsp (cinnamon, coriander, mustard seed, allspice, cloves, peppercorns).

**The Spice Rub:**
- **Coarse Black Pepper:** 4 tbsp.
- **Coriander Seeds (toasted and crushed):** 3 tbsp.
- **Garlic Powder:** 1 tbsp.
- **Mustard Powder:** 1 tbsp.

**The Sandwich:**
- **Rye Bread:** 2 thick slices (seeded preferred).
- **Spicy Brown Mustard:** 1-2 tbsp.
- **Half-Sour Pickle:** On the side.

### Instructions
1. **The Brine:** Combine water, salt, sugar, curing salt, garlic, and pickling spice in a large pot. Bring to a boil until solids dissolve. Cool completely.
2. **The Cure:** Submerge the beef in the brine. Refrigerate for 7-10 days. Flip the meat every few days.
3. **The Rinse:** Remove beef from brine and rinse thoroughly under cold water. Pat dry.
4. **The Rub:** Combine the spice rub ingredients and press firmly into all sides of the beef.
5. **The Smoke:** Smoke at 225°F (107°C) for 3-4 hours until the internal temperature reaches 150°F (65°C).
6. **The Steam (The Secret):** This is what makes Carnegie Deli's pastrami melt-in-your-mouth. Place the smoked meat in a steamer basket over simmering water. Cover tightly and steam for another 3-4 hours until the internal temperature reaches 200°F (93°C).
7. **The Slice:** Hand-slice against the grain into thin, warm ribbons.
8. **The Assembly:** Stack the warm pastrami at least 4 inches high on rye bread. Apply mustard to one side. Serve with a pickle.

### Secret Tips
- **The Navel Cut:** While brisket is common, Carnegie Deli famously uses the "Navel" (beef plate), which is fattier and more tender.
- **Steaming is Key:** Most home cooks stop at the smoking stage. Steaming breaks down the tough connective tissues and renders the fat perfectly, creating that "Mile High" texture.
- **Hand-Slicing:** Never use a machine. Hand-slicing keeps the meat from falling apart and preserves the juice.

### Origin Story
The Carnegie Deli opened in 1937 directly across from Carnegie Hall. It became world-famous for its massive portions and its appearance in Woody Allen’s *Broadway Danny Rose*. The "Woody Allen" sandwich (a combo of pastrami and corned beef) was their most famous order.
