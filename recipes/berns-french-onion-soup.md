# Bern's Style French Onion Soup

Bern's Steak House in Tampa, Florida, is world-renowned for its legendary wine cellar and its meticulous approach to food. Their French Onion Soup is a labor of love that takes days to prepare, resulting in a depth of flavor that is unmatched by any standard recipe.

## Ingredients

### The Multi-Day Beef & Veal Stock
- **2 kg** Beef marrow bones and veal bones
- **500g** Beef trimmings (ribeye or sirloin scraps)
- **2** Large carrots, chopped
- **2** Celery stalks, chopped
- **2** Large onions, halved
- **1 cup** Tomato paste
- **10 liters** Water
- **4** Bay leaves
- **1 bunch** Fresh thyme

### The Caramelized Onions
- **5 kg** Yellow onions (about 12-15 large onions)
- **1/2 cup** Unsalted butter
- **1 tsp** Salt

### The Soup
- **4 liters** Prepared Bern's stock
- **All** Caramelized onions
- **1/2 cup** Dry Sherry (High quality)
- **To taste** Salt and freshly ground black pepper

### The Topping
- **As needed** Day-old baguette, sliced into 1-inch rounds
- **As needed** Sliced Gruyère cheese
- **As needed** Sliced Provolone cheese
- **As needed** Freshly grated Parmesan cheese

## Instructions

### 1. The Stock (Day 1 & 2)
1. Roast the bones and beef trimmings at 425°F (220°C) until deep brown.
2. In a massive stockpot, sear the vegetables, add tomato paste and cook for 2 minutes.
3. Add the roasted bones and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a very low simmer.
4. **Secret Technique:** Simmer for at least 48 hours. Skim the fat and impurities regularly. The stock should reduce significantly and become gelatinous.
5. Strain through a fine-mesh sieve and discard solids. Chill overnight to remove all solidified fat from the surface.

### 2. The Caramelized Onions (Day 2)
1. Peel and slice the 5kg of onions thinly.
2. Melt butter in a large, heavy-bottomed pot or slow cooker.
3. Add onions and salt. 
4. **Secret Technique:** Cook on very low heat for at least 12-18 hours. Bern's uses steam-jacketed kettles for a consistent, slow caramelization that turns the onions a deep, dark mahogany without ever burning them. They should be sweet and jam-like.

### 3. The Marriage (Day 3)
1. Add the caramelized onions to the prepared stock.
2. Bring to a gentle simmer for 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld.
3. Add the dry sherry and season with salt and pepper.

### 4. The Finish
1. Ladle the soup into oven-safe crocks. 
2. Place a toasted baguette round on top.
3. **Secret Technique:** Layer the cheese. Use a slice of Provolone for stretch, a generous amount of Gruyère for nutty flavor, and a sprinkle of Parmesan for a salty crust.
4. Place under the broiler until the cheese is bubbling and has deep brown "tiger spots."

## Secret Tips
- **The Onion Volume:** It may seem like too many onions, but they will reduce by 80% during the long caramelization process.
- **The Stock Base:** Do not use store-bought broth. The gelatin from the veal bones is what gives Bern's soup its signature "mouthfeel."
- **No Herbs in the Soup:** Notice there are no herbs added to the final soup. The 48-hour stock provides all the herbal notes needed from the initial simmer.

## Origin
Founded in 1956 by Bern and Gert Laxer, **Bern's Steak House** is a Tampa icon. Their French Onion Soup remains one of the most ordered items, a testament to the fact that there are no shortcuts to greatness.
