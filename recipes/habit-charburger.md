# The Habit Burger Grill Charburger

The award-winning char-grilled favorite: a seasoned beef patty cooked over an open flame for that distinct smoky flavor, served with caramelized onions and fresh toppings.

### Ingredients
- **Ground Beef (80/20):** 85g (3oz)
- **Mayonnaise:** 15g (1 tbsp)
- **Caramelized Onions:** 15g (1 tbsp, diced and sautéed until soft)
- **Dill Pickle Slices:** 3 slices
- **Tomato:** 1 slice
- **Iceberg Lettuce:** Shredded
- **Sesame Seed Bun:** 1 bun
- **Salt & Pepper:** To taste

### Instructions
1. **Prep the Onions:** Finely dice a yellow onion and sauté in a pan with a little oil and a pinch of salt over medium-low heat until soft and light brown (about 15-20 minutes).
2. **The Char-Grill:** 
   - Preheat your grill to high heat (or a cast iron grill pan). 
   - Form the beef into a thin patty. 
   - Season generously with salt and pepper.
   - Grill over an open flame for 2-3 minutes per side until you get distinct char marks and a smoky aroma.
3. **Toast the Bun:** Toast the sesame seed bun on the grill or in a pan until warm and slightly crisp.
4. **Assembly (Bottom to Top):**
   - Bottom Bun
   - Mayonnaise
   - Dill Pickles
   - Tomato Slice
   - Shredded Iceberg Lettuce
   - Char-grilled Patty
   - Caramelized Onions
   - Top Bun

### Secret Tips
- **Open Flame:** The "Char" in Charburger comes from the open flame. If you don't have a grill, a very hot cast iron grill pan is the best substitute to get those char marks.
- **Seasoning:** Don't be shy with the salt; it helps create the crust that carries the smoky flavor.
- **Simplicity:** Unlike In-N-Out or Shake Shack, The Habit relies on the quality of the char-grill rather than a complex secret sauce.

### Origin Story
The Habit Burger Grill was founded in 1969 in Santa Barbara, California. It was originally a family-owned business that focused on high-quality ingredients and the "char-grilling" technique. In 2014, Consumer Reports named the Charburger the best burger in America, catapulting the chain to national fame.
