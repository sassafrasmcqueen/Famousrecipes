# NYC-Style Bodega Egg & Cheese on a Roll

The quintessential New York breakfast: fast, greasy, and incredibly satisfying. Usually ordered as a "Bacon, Egg, and Cheese" (BEC) but the base Egg & Cheese is the foundation.

### Ingredients
- **Kaiser Roll:** 1 fresh, crusty roll (must be a New York Kaiser roll).
- **Large Eggs:** 2.
- **American Cheese:** 2 slices (Land O'Lakes is the gold standard).
- **Unsalted Butter:** 15g (1 tbsp).
- **Salt & Pepper:** To taste.
- **Ketchup:** Optional (as in "Salt-Pepper-Ketchup").
- **Hot Sauce:** Optional (standard red hot sauce).

### Instructions
1. **Prep the Roll:** Slice the Kaiser roll in half. Butter the cut sides generously and toast them on a hot griddle or large pan until golden and slightly crisp. Set aside.
2. **Cook the Eggs:** Melt the remaining butter on the griddle. Crack two eggs. For a standard bodega style, break the yolks and scramble them lightly directly on the griddle, forming a rectangular shape roughly the size of the roll.
3. **Melt the Cheese:** Once the eggs are 80% cooked, place the two slices of American cheese on top. 
4. **The Steam (The Secret):** Place the toasted top bun directly onto the melting cheese. Squirt a tiny splash of water onto the griddle next to the eggs and immediately cover the eggs and the bun with a lid or a metal cloche for 30 seconds. This steams the cheese and softens the bun.
5. **Assembly:** Use a wide spatula to scoop the egg and cheese stack onto the bottom bun. 
6. **The Wrap:** Fold the sandwich into a square of aluminum foil or wax paper. 
7. **The Rest:** Let the wrapped sandwich sit for 1 minute before eating. This final "rest" in the foil allows the flavors to meld and the bread to reach the perfect pillowy texture.

### Secret Tips
- **American Cheese Only:** Do not use cheddar or swiss. The plastic-like melt of processed American cheese is essential for the authentic taste.
- **The Foil Wrap:** The most overlooked step. Wrapping it in foil for a minute creates a micro-steam environment that transforms the roll from "bread with eggs" into a cohesive NYC Bodega sandwich.
- **SPK:** When the cook asks "Salt-Pepper-Ketchup?", the answer is usually "Yes."

### Origin Story
The NYC bodega (or deli) is the lifeblood of the city. While eggs on rolls have been around for a century, the specific "griddle-scrambled, cheese-steamed" technique became the standard fast-food breakfast for commuters and construction workers in the mid-20th century.
