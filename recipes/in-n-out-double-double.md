# In-N-Out Double-Double (Animal Style)

The California classic: two beef patties, two slices of cheese, and the legendary "Animal Style" treatment featuring mustard-grilled patties and caramelized onions.

### Ingredients
**The Spread:**
- **Mayonnaise:** 30g (2 tbsp)
- **Ketchup:** 15g (1 tbsp)
- **Sweet Pickle Relish:** 10g (2 tsp)
- **Sugar:** 2g (1/2 tsp)
- **White Vinegar:** 2ml (1/2 tsp)

**The Burger:**
- **Ground Beef (80/20):** 115g total (divided into two 57g/2oz patties)
- **American Cheese:** 2 slices
- **Yellow Mustard:** 10g (for grilling)
- **Onion:** 1/2 medium onion, finely diced (caramelized)
- **Tomato:** 2 thick slices
- **Iceberg Lettuce:** Hand-leafed, enough to cover the bun
- **Sponge Dough Bun:** 1 bun, split
- **Salt & Pepper:** To taste

### Instructions
1. **Caramelize Onions:** Sauté the finely diced onions in a pan with a little oil over low heat for 30-40 minutes until deep brown and sweet. Add a splash of water if they dry out. Set aside.
2. **Make the Spread:** Combine mayo, ketchup, relish, sugar, and vinegar in a small bowl. Whisk until smooth.
3. **Toast the Bun:** In-N-Out toasts their buns for a long time until they are very dark and crisp on the cut side. Use a dry pan or a tiny bit of butter.
4. **Mustard-Grill the Patties:** 
   - Form the beef into two thin patties slightly larger than the bun.
   - Season one side with salt and pepper.
   - Place patties on a hot griddle, seasoned side down. 
   - Spread 1 tsp of yellow mustard on the raw top side of each patty.
   - After 1-2 minutes, flip the patties so the mustard side hits the griddle.
5. **Add Cheese:** Immediately place a slice of American cheese on each patty.
6. **Assembly (Bottom to Top):**
   - Bottom Bun
   - Generous layer of Spread
   - 2 Slices of Tomato
   - Iceberg Lettuce (tucked neatly)
   - First Patty with Cheese
   - Second Patty with Cheese
   - Caramelized Onions
   - Top Bun

### Secret Tips
- **The Bun Toast:** The "crunch" of an In-N-Out burger comes from the bun. It must be toasted until dark brown.
- **Cold Produce:** Use cold, crisp iceberg lettuce and cold tomato slices to contrast with the hot beef.
- **The Mustard Flip:** Smearing mustard on the raw side before flipping creates a savory, tangy crust that is the hallmark of Animal Style.

### Origin Story
In-N-Out Burger was founded in 1948 by Harry and Esther Snyder in Baldwin Park, California. It was the first drive-thru hamburger stand in the state. The "Animal Style" burger was created in 1961 by customers and eventually became the most famous item on the "Secret Menu."
