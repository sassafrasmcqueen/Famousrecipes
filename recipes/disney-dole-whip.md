# Disney's Pineapple Dole Whip (Copycat)

The refreshing, tropical soft-serve that has reached cult-status at Disney Parks around the world.

### Ingredients
- **Frozen Pineapple Chunks:** 2 cups (approx. 450g).
- **Pineapple Juice:** 1/2 cup (120ml), chilled.
- **Vanilla Ice Cream:** 1 large scoop (or 1/2 cup vanilla coconut milk ice cream for a dairy-free version).
- **Lemon Juice:** 1 tsp (optional, for extra tang).
- **Pinch of Salt:** Just a tiny bit to enhance the pineapple flavor.

### Instructions
1. **Freeze:** If starting with fresh pineapple, cut into chunks and freeze for at least 4 hours.
2. **Combine:** Place the frozen pineapple, pineapple juice, and vanilla ice cream into a high-powered blender.
3. **Blend:** Start on low speed and gradually increase. Use a tamper if necessary to keep the frozen fruit moving. Blend until completely smooth and thick.
4. **Consistency Check:** If it's too thick to blend, add more pineapple juice 1 tablespoon at a time. It should be the consistency of soft-serve ice cream.
5. **Serve:** For the authentic Disney look, transfer the mixture to a piping bag fitted with a large star tip. Pipe into a tall swirl in a cup.
6. **Garnish:** Top with a small pineapple wedge or a maraschino cherry.

### Secret Tips
- **Keep it Frozen:** The key to the texture is using frozen pineapple rather than adding ice, which would water down the flavor.
- **The Dairy Choice:** While the modern Disney Dole Whip is actually vegan (using a dry mix), the most accurate home-copycat flavor uses vanilla ice cream or a creamy coconut-based vanilla alternative.
- **The Juice Balance:** Don't add too much juice at once, or you'll end up with a smoothie instead of a soft-serve.

### Origin Story
Dole Whip was created by the Dole Food Company in 1984. It was first introduced at the Enchanted Tiki Room in Disneyland as a sponsorship deal. Originally, it contained dairy, but the recipe was changed to be vegan-friendly in the early 2000s, contributing even further to its massive popularity.
