# IKEA Swedish Meatballs (Official Copycat)

The comforting taste of the world's favorite furniture store cafeteria, complete with the signature cream sauce.

### Ingredients
**Meatballs:**
- **Beef Mince:** 500g.
- **Pork Mince:** 250g.
- **Onion:** 1 medium, finely chopped.
- **Garlic:** 1 clove, crushed or minced.
- **Breadcrumbs:** 100g.
- **Egg:** 1 large.
- **Milk:** 5 tbsp (whole milk preferred).
- **Seasoning:** Generous pinch of salt and pepper.

**Cream Sauce (Gravy):**
- **Butter:** 40g.
- **All-Purpose Flour:** 40g.
- **Vegetable Stock:** 150ml.
- **Beef Stock:** 150ml.
- **Double Cream:** 150ml.
- **Soy Sauce:** 2 tsp.
- **Dijon Mustard:** 1 tsp.

### Instructions
1. **Mix Meatballs:** Combine beef and pork mince in a bowl. Add chopped onion, garlic, breadcrumbs, egg, and milk. Mix thoroughly with your hands until well combined.
2. **Shape:** Roll the mixture into small, round balls (about the size of a golf ball).
3. **Chill:** Place the meatballs on a clean plate, cover, and store in the fridge for **2 hours**. This is crucial to help them hold their shape during cooking.
4. **Sear:** Heat a little oil in a frying pan over medium heat. When hot, gently add the meatballs and brown them on all sides.
5. **Bake:** Once browned, transfer to an ovenproof dish and cover. Place in a preheated oven at **350°F (180°C)** and cook for a further **30 minutes**.
6. **Prepare Sauce:** Melt the butter in a pan. Whisk in the flour and cook for 2 minutes to create a roux. Gradually add the vegetable and beef stocks, whisking continuously.
7. **Finish Sauce:** Add the double cream, soy sauce, and Dijon mustard. Bring to a simmer and allow the sauce to thicken.
8. **Serve:** Serve the meatballs with the cream sauce, creamy mashed potatoes, and a dollop of lingonberry jam.

### Secret Tips
- **The 2:1 Ratio:** IKEA uses a specific ratio of beef to pork (roughly 2 parts beef to 1 part pork) to achieve the perfect balance of flavor and tenderness.
- **Patience is Key:** Don't skip the 2-hour chill time. It prevents the meatballs from falling apart in the pan.
- **The Umami Boost:** The combination of soy sauce and Dijon mustard in the gravy is the "secret" that gives it that specific savory depth.

### Origin Story
While meatballs are a traditional Swedish dish (*köttbullar*), the IKEA version became a global icon after being introduced in IKEA stores in the 1980s. Founder Ingvar Kamprad famously said, "It's difficult to do business with someone on an empty stomach." Today, IKEA sells over 1 billion meatballs annually.
