# Ruth's Chris Style Creamed Spinach

A legendary side dish that has a cult following. Ruth's Chris version is known for being incredibly smooth, rich, and perfectly seasoned with a subtle hint of warmth.

## Ingredients

- **900g (2 lbs)** Frozen chopped spinach, thawed
- **4 tbsp** Unsalted butter
- **4 tbsp** All-purpose flour
- **1/2 cup** Yellow onion, minced very fine
- **1 clove** Garlic, minced
- **2 cups** Whole milk
- **1/2 cup** Heavy cream
- **1/2 tsp** Ground nutmeg (Freshly grated is best)
- **1/2 tsp** White pepper
- **1 tsp** Salt (Adjust to taste)

## Instructions

### 1. The Spinach Prep
1. **Secret Technique:** Place the thawed spinach in a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth. Squeeze with all your might until every drop of water is removed. The spinach should be a dry, crumbly mass. 
2. Chop the dried spinach one more time to ensure no large stems remain.

### 2. The White Sauce (Béchamel)
1. Melt butter in a large saucepan over medium heat.
2. Add the finely minced onion and cook until translucent but not browned. Add garlic for the last 30 seconds.
3. Whisk in the flour to create a roux. Cook for 2 minutes to remove the raw flour taste, but keep it a light blonde color.
4. Slowly pour in the milk and heavy cream, whisking constantly to prevent lumps.
5. Simmer for 5-7 minutes until thickened and silky.

### 3. Seasoning
1. **Secret Technique:** Add the salt, white pepper, and nutmeg. The nutmeg is the "hidden" ingredient that gives the dish its classic steakhouse profile. White pepper is used instead of black to maintain the pristine cream color.

### 4. Integration
1. Add the dry chopped spinach to the cream sauce.
2. Fold gently until completely incorporated.
3. Simmer on low for another 5 minutes to allow the spinach to absorb the cream. If it's too thick, add a splash more milk.

## Secret Tips
- **The "Super Squeeze":** If the spinach is even slightly wet, your creamed spinach will turn into a watery, green mess. This is the most common mistake.
- **Onion Texture:** The onions should be minced so finely they practically melt into the sauce. You want their flavor, not their crunch.
- **Constant Heat:** Keep the sauce on a low simmer. High heat can cause the dairy to break or the bottom to scorch.

## Origin
**Ruth's Chris Steak House** was founded by Ruth Fertel in 1965 in New Orleans. Her "Chris Steak House" became a global phenomenon, but she never lost the New Orleans flair for rich, decadent sides. This creamed spinach has remained unchanged on the menu for over 50 years.
