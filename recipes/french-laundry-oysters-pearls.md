# Oysters and Pearls (Thomas Keller's Signature Dish)

Bringing the iconic "sabayon of pearl tapioca with Beau Soleil oysters and white sturgeon caviar" from The French Laundry and Per Se to your home kitchen.

## Ingredients

### Pearl Tapioca Sabayon
- **50g** Small pearl tapioca (preferably Sabago brand)
- **240ml** Whole milk, cold
- **240ml** Heavy cream, cold
- **2** Large egg yolks
- **60ml** Crème fraîche
- **To taste** Kosher salt and freshly ground white pepper

### Oysters and Poaching Liquid
- **12** Small oysters (such as Kumamoto or Beau Soleil)
- **60ml** Dry white wine (Sauvignon Blanc or Chablis)
- **60ml** Oyster liquor (reserved from shucking)
- **15g** Shallots, minced
- **15g** Unsalted butter
- **1 tsp** Lemon juice

### To Serve
- **30g** White sturgeon caviar (high quality)
- **1 tbsp** Fresh chives, finely snipped

## Instructions

### 1. Prepare the Tapioca
1. Soak the tapioca in the milk for at least 1 hour (or overnight in the refrigerator).
2. Transfer the tapioca and milk to a small saucepan. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly, until the tapioca is translucent and tender (about 15-20 minutes). Do not let it boil.
3. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg yolks. Gradually whisk in about 1/4 cup of the warm tapioca mixture to temper the yolks, then return the yolk mixture to the saucepan.
4. Cook over very low heat for another 2 minutes, stirring constantly, until slightly thickened. 
5. Remove from heat and stir in the crème fraîche. Season with salt and white pepper. Keep warm in a double boiler or a very low oven.

### 2. Poach the Oysters
1. In a small sauté pan, melt the butter over medium-low heat. Add the minced shallots and sweat until translucent but not browned.
2. Add the white wine and oyster liquor. Bring to a gentle simmer and reduce by half.
3. Add the shucked oysters to the liquid. Poach very gently for about 1 minute, just until the edges begin to curl. Do not overcook. 
4. Remove the oysters from the liquid and keep warm. Strain the liquid and add a teaspoon of lemon juice.

### 3. Final Sabayon Consistency
1. In a chilled bowl, whip the heavy cream to soft peaks.
2. Gently fold the whipped cream into the warm tapioca mixture. The result should be a light, airy, and "pearl-like" sabayon.

### 4. Assembly
1. Place a generous spoonful of the tapioca sabayon into the bottom of a small, warmed shell or a glass dish.
2. Place two poached oysters on top of the sabayon.
3. Spoon a small amount of the poaching liquid over the oysters.
4. Top with a generous "quenelle" (dollop) of sturgeon caviar.
5. Garnish with snipped chives.

## Secret Tips
- **The Tapioca Texture:** The key to this dish is the "pearl" texture. Using high-quality small pearl tapioca and soaking it in milk ensures it becomes tender without losing its shape.
- **The Sabayon:** Folding in the whipped cream at the very last second is what gives the sabayon its signature light, ethereal texture.
- **Oyster Liquor:** Always save the liquid when shucking the oysters; it carries the essential "ocean" flavor that seasons the entire dish.

## Origin
First served at **The French Laundry** in Yountville, California, "Oysters and Pearls" is Thomas Keller's most famous opening course. It is a play on words (the pearls in the tapioca and the pearls in the oysters) and a masterclass in balancing luxury ingredients (caviar) with humble ones (tapioca). It has been a permanent fixture on the menu at both The French Laundry and Per Se since they opened.
