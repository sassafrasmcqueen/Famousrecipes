# Din Tai Fung Style Spicy Shrimp & Pork Wontons

Famous for their delicate skins and explosive chili oil sauce, these wontons from Din Tai Fung are a masterclass in balance. The sauce is the true star—sweet, tangy, savory, and spicy.

## Ingredients

### The Wontons
- **300g** Ground pork (80/20 fat ratio)
- **200g** Shrimp, peeled, deveined, and minced
- **1 tsp** Ginger, finely grated
- **2** Green onions, finely minced
- **1 tbsp** Soy sauce
- **1 tsp** Sesame oil
- **1/2 tsp** White pepper
- **1 pack** Wonton wrappers (thin/Hong Kong style)

### The House Special Spicy Sauce
- **3 tbsp** Chili oil (must include the crispy sediment/flakes)
- **2 tbsp** Light soy sauce
- **1 tbsp** Chinkiang black vinegar
- **1 tbsp** Granulated sugar
- **1 tsp** Sesame oil
- **2 cloves** Garlic, finely minced
- **1 tsp** Ginger, finely minced

### Garnish
- Fresh cilantro, chopped
- Green onions, sliced thinly on a bias

## Instructions

### 1. Prepare the Filling
1. In a large bowl, mix ground pork, minced shrimp, ginger, green onions, soy sauce, sesame oil, and white pepper.
2. Stir vigorously in one direction for 2-3 minutes until the mixture becomes sticky and "bouncy" (a traditional Chinese technique).

### 2. Fold the Wontons
1. Place 1 teaspoon of filling in the center of a wrapper.
2. Moisten the edges with water. Fold into a triangle, then bring the two bottom corners together and press to seal (creating a "nurse's cap" or "envelope" shape).
3. Ensure no air is trapped inside to prevent bursting.

### 3. Make the Spicy Sauce
1. In a small bowl, whisk together chili oil, light soy sauce, black vinegar, sugar, sesame oil, garlic, and ginger.
2. **Secret Tip:** Whisk until the sugar is completely dissolved. The balance of sugar and black vinegar is what creates that addictive "Din Tai Fung" profile.

### 4. Cook the Wontons
1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. 
2. Gently drop the wontons in. When they float to the surface, cook for another 1-2 minutes until the skins are translucent and the filling is cooked through.
3. Use a slotted spoon to transfer the wontons directly into serving bowls.

### 5. Assembly
1. Pour a generous amount of the spicy sauce over the hot wontons.
2. Garnish with plenty of cilantro and green onions.
3. Toss gently before eating to ensure every wonton is coated in the chili oil sediment.

## Secret Tips
- **The Chili Oil:** Use a high-quality chili oil that has lots of "bits" at the bottom. The toasted garlic and chili flakes in the oil provide the necessary texture.
- **Black Vinegar:** Do not substitute with white vinegar or balsamic. Chinkiang black vinegar has a unique smoky, malty flavor essential to this dish.
- **Shrimp Texture:** Don't turn the shrimp into a paste. Mincing them by hand gives the wontons a much better "snap."

## Origin
**Din Tai Fung** originated in Taiwan in 1958 as a cooking oil retail shop. In 1972, it transitioned into a steamed dumpling and noodle restaurant. It is now world-renowned, with its Hong Kong branch awarded a Michelin star for five consecutive years.
