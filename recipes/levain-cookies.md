# Levain Bakery Chocolate Chip Cookies (Copycat)

The legendary NYC cookie: massive, thick, and gooey on the inside with a crisp, golden exterior.

### Ingredients
- **Cold Butter:** 225g (1 cup) unsalted butter, cubed into small pieces.
- **Brown Sugar:** 150g (3/4 cup) packed light brown sugar.
- **Granulated Sugar:** 100g (1/2 cup) white sugar.
- **Eggs:** 2 large eggs, cold.
- **Cake Flour:** 190g (1 1/2 cups) - provides the tender, scone-like texture.
- **All-Purpose Flour:** 190g (1 1/2 cups).
- **Cornstarch:** 1 tsp - helps with thickness and tenderness.
- **Baking Soda:** 1 tsp.
- **Salt:** 1/2 tsp.
- **Chocolate Chips:** 340g (2 cups) semi-sweet chocolate chips (larger chips preferred).
- **Walnuts:** 225g (2 cups) walnuts, roughly chopped.

### Instructions
1. **Prep:** Preheat oven to **415°F (212°C)**. Line two large baking sheets with parchment paper.
2. **Cream Butter & Sugars:** In a stand mixer with a paddle attachment, cream the *cold* cubed butter and both sugars for about 2-3 minutes until combined but not overly fluffy. You want to keep the butter cool.
3. **Add Eggs:** Add eggs one at a time, mixing just until incorporated.
4. **Dry Ingredients:** Add both flours, cornstarch, baking soda, and salt. Mix on low speed until just a few streaks of flour remain. Do not overmix.
5. **Add-ins:** Fold in the chocolate chips and walnuts by hand or on the lowest mixer setting.
6. **Portion:** Divide the dough into 6 large mounds (approx 170g or 6oz each). Do *not* roll them into smooth balls; keep them rough and craggy for that signature Levain look.
7. **Chill (Optional but recommended):** Chill the mounds in the freezer for 15 minutes before baking to ensure they don't spread too much.
8. **Bake:** Space the mounds far apart (only 3-4 per sheet). Bake for **10-12 minutes** until the tops are golden brown but the centers still look slightly underbaked.
9. **Cool:** Let the cookies rest on the baking sheet for at least 15-20 minutes. They will continue to set as they cool.

### Secret Tips
- **Cold Butter is Non-Negotiable:** Using cold, cubed butter instead of room temperature butter prevents the cookies from spreading and creates the signature thick, dense texture.
- **High Temperature:** Baking at 415°F creates an immediate crust on the outside while leaving the center perfectly gooey.
- **The "No-Roll" Technique:** Don't press or smooth the dough balls. The craggy surface creates more texture and those iconic golden peaks.

### Origin Story
Levain Bakery was founded in 1995 on West 74th Street in Manhattan by Connie McDonald and Pamela Weekes. Originally a bread bakery, they created the "World Famous Chocolate Chip Walnut Cookie" to fuel their triathlon training. It quickly became a global sensation, known for being a "six-ounce mountain of a cookie."
