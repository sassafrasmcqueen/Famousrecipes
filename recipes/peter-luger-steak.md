# Peter Luger Style 'Steak for Two' (Porterhouse)

The definitive Brooklyn steakhouse experience. This recipe focuses on the technical precision required to mimic the Peter Luger method of dry-aging, high-heat broiling, and the iconic sizzling butter finish.

## Ingredients

- **1 (36-40 oz)** USDA Prime Porterhouse steak (at least 1.5 to 2 inches thick)
- **To taste** Coarse Kosher salt
- **4 tbsp** Clarified butter (Ghee)
- **Optional** Rendered beef suet (fat trimmings from the steak)

## Instructions

### 1. The Setup (Dry-Aging)
1. **Secret Technique:** While Peter Luger dry-ages in-house for 28+ days, you can mimic this at home using an UMAi Dry bag or by placing the steak on a wire rack in a dedicated fridge for 3-7 days. This concentrates the beef flavor and tenderizes the muscle fibers.
2. Ensure the steak is at room temperature for at least 60 minutes before cooking. Pat extremely dry with paper towels.

### 2. Seasoning
1. Season generously with Kosher salt on all sides. Peter Luger famously uses only salt—no pepper or rubs—to let the aged beef flavor dominate.

### 3. The Broil
1. Preheat your broiler to its highest setting (usually 500°F-550°F). If you have an outdoor infrared broiler (like a Beefer), even better.
2. Place the steak on a cold broiler pan. Position it as close to the heating element as possible (2-3 inches).
3. Broil for 4-6 minutes per side for medium-rare, or until a deep, dark crust (the Maillard reaction) forms.

### 4. The Sizzling Finish
1. Remove the steak and let it rest for 5-10 minutes. 
2. **Secret Technique:** Slice the steak away from the bone (strip side and filet side). Slice the sections into thick strips.
3. Place a heavy ceramic or cast-iron platter in the oven/broiler until it is smoking hot.
4. Place the bone back in the center of the hot platter and arrange the sliced steak around it.
5. Drop 4 tablespoons of clarified butter (and a few pieces of rendered beef fat) onto the platter. It should sizzle violently.
6. Return to the broiler for exactly 60 seconds to "finish" the slices in the bubbling fat.

### 5. Service
1. Tilt the platter slightly by placing a spoon under one side. The butter and beef juices will collect at the bottom. 
2. Spoon this "holy water" back over the steak slices before serving.

## Secret Tips
- **The Cut:** You must use a Porterhouse, not just a T-bone. A Porterhouse has a significantly larger tenderloin (filet) section.
- **Clarified Butter:** Regular butter has milk solids that will burn at these temperatures. Clarified butter (or Ghee) provides the richness without the bitter burnt taste.
- **The Tilt:** Tilting the plate isn't just for show; it allows the steak to stay out of the pool of grease so the crust stays crisp, while providing a reservoir of flavor to spoon over.

## Origin
Opened in 1887 in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, **Peter Luger Steak House** is an American institution. Their uncompromising approach to meat selection and aging has made their Porterhouse the gold standard by which all other steakhouses are measured.
