# The Halal Guys Style Chicken and Rice

The definitive NYC street food experience. This copycat recipe captures the aromatic yellow rice, the perfectly charred chicken, and most importantly, the legendary white sauce that brings it all together.

## Ingredients

### The Chicken
- **900g** Boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into 1-inch pieces
- **2 tbsp** Lemon juice
- **1 tbsp** Dried oregano
- **1/2 tsp** Ground coriander
- **1/2 tsp** Ground cumin
- **2 cloves** Garlic, minced
- **2 tbsp** Olive oil
- **To taste** Salt and freshly ground black pepper

### The Rice
- **2 cups** Basmati rice
- **2 tbsp** Unsalted butter
- **1 tsp** Ground turmeric
- **1/2 tsp** Ground cumin
- **4 cups** Chicken stock (low sodium)
- **1/2 tsp** Salt

### The White Sauce (The Legend)
- **1/2 cup** Mayonnaise (Hellman's/Best Foods preferred)
- **1/4 cup** Greek yogurt (unflavored, full fat)
- **1 tbsp** Sugar
- **1 tbsp** White vinegar
- **1 tsp** Lemon juice
- **1/2 tsp** Freshly ground black pepper
- **1 tbsp** Dried parsley

### The Red Sauce (Fiery)
- **1/4 cup** Harissa paste or ground red chili paste
- **1 tbsp** White vinegar
- **1 clove** Garlic, minced

## Instructions

### 1. Marinate the Chicken
1. In a large bowl, combine lemon juice, oregano, coriander, cumin, garlic, and olive oil.
2. Add chicken pieces and toss to coat. 
3. Marinate in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour, or up to 4 hours.

### 2. Cook the Rice
1. Melt butter in a large pot over medium heat. 
2. Add turmeric and cumin, stirring for 30 seconds until fragrant.
3. Add the rice and stir to coat each grain in the spiced butter.
4. Pour in the chicken stock and salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a low simmer.
5. Cover and cook for 15-20 minutes until the liquid is absorbed. Remove from heat and let sit, covered, for 5 minutes before fluffing with a fork.

### 3. Sear the Chicken
1. Heat a large heavy-bottomed skillet (cast iron is best) over medium-high heat with a splash of oil.
2. Add chicken in a single layer (work in batches if needed).
3. Cook until deep golden brown and slightly charred on the edges (about 8-10 minutes total).

### 4. Make the White Sauce
1. Whisk together the mayonnaise, yogurt, sugar, vinegar, lemon juice, pepper, and parsley in a small bowl.
2. **Secret Tip:** Let the sauce chill in the fridge for at least 30 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld and the sugar to dissolve completely.

### 5. Assembly
1. Fill a plate with a generous bed of yellow rice.
2. Top with the seared chicken.
3. Serve with a side of shredded iceberg lettuce and diced tomatoes.
4. Drizzle the white sauce liberally over everything. Use the red sauce sparingly—it's explosive!

## Secret Tips
- **The Chicken Char:** Don't crowd the pan. You want high heat to get those crispy, charred bits that mimic the vertical spit-roasting at the street carts.
- **The White Sauce Base:** The Halal Guys sauce is famously mayo-based. The addition of Greek yogurt and vinegar provides the necessary tang to cut through the richness.
- **Rice Texture:** Rinsing the basmati rice until the water runs clear is essential for long, separate grains that don't clump.

## Origin
Founded in 1990 as a simple hot dog cart on 53rd Street and 6th Avenue in Manhattan, **The Halal Guys** pivoted to serving halal platters to Muslim taxi drivers. It exploded into a global phenomenon, becoming synonymous with the NYC street food scene.
