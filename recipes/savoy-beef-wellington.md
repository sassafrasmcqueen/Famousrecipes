# Gordon Ramsay's Beef Wellington (Savoy Grill Standard)

The crown jewel of Gordon Ramsay's Savoy Grill, this dish is a masterclass in precision, timing, and luxury. It features tender beef fillet, earthy mushroom duxelles, salty Parma ham, and buttery, golden puff pastry.

## Ingredients

### Beef and Duxelles
- **2 x 400g** Beef fillets (center-cut larder trimmed)
- **500g** Mixture of wild mushrooms (chestnut, cremini, shiitake), cleaned
- **1** Thyme sprig (leaves only)
- **8 slices** Parma ham
- **500g** Puff pastry (high-quality all-butter)
- **2** egg yolks, beaten with 1 tbsp water and a pinch of salt (egg wash)
- **To taste** Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper
- **As needed** Olive oil for frying

### Red Wine Sauce (Jus)
- **2 tbsp** Olive oil
- **200g** Beef trimmings (reserved from trimming the fillets)
- **4** Large shallots, peeled and sliced
- **12** Black peppercorns
- **1** Bay leaf
- **1** Thyme sprig
- **Splash** Red wine vinegar
- **1 x 750ml** Bottle red wine
- **750ml** Beef stock (high-quality veal or beef)

## Instructions

### 1. Prepare the Beef
1. Season the beef fillets with salt and pepper.
2. Heat a heavy-bottomed pan with olive oil until smoking hot. Sear the beef for 30-60 seconds on all sides until browned but rare in the middle.
3. Immediately remove from the pan and let cool. Wrap tightly in a triple layer of cling film to set its shape into a perfect cylinder. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour (or overnight).

### 2. The Mushroom Duxelles
1. Finely chop the mushrooms (you can use a food processor, but do not turn them into a paste).
2. Dry-fry the mushrooms in a hot pan with the thyme leaves and a pinch of salt. 
3. **Secret Tip:** Cook until all the moisture has evaporated and the mushrooms are dry and concentrated in flavor. This "dry" duxelles is crucial to prevent a soggy bottom. Remove and cool completely.

### 3. Wrapping the Wellington
1. Lay a large sheet of cling film on a work surface. Arrange 4 slices of Parma ham in the middle, slightly overlapping, to create a square.
2. Spread half the cooled mushroom duxelles evenly over the ham.
3. Unwrap the chilled beef and place it in the center. Use the cling film to roll the ham and mushrooms tightly around the beef. Tie the ends of the cling film to create a tight "log." Repeat for the second fillet.
4. Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to set.

### 4. Pastry and Baking
1. Roll out the puff pastry into a rectangle large enough to envelop the beef.
2. Brush the edges with egg wash. Unwrap the beef log and place it in the center. 
3. Roll the pastry around the beef, trim any excess, and seal the edges. 
4. Wrap tightly in cling film again and chill for another 30 minutes.
5. Preheat the oven to 200°C (400°F).
6. Unwrap the Wellington, brush the entire surface with egg wash, and score lightly with a knife for decoration.
7. Bake for 15-20 minutes until the pastry is golden brown. Internal temperature should be 48-52°C for medium-rare.
8. **Resting is Mandatory:** Let the Wellington rest for at least 10 minutes before carving.

### 5. Red Wine Sauce
1. Heat oil in a large pan and fry beef trimmings until browned.
2. Add shallots, peppercorns, bay, and thyme. Cook until shallots are caramelized.
3. Deglaze with red wine vinegar and reduce until dry.
4. Add the red wine and reduce by half.
5. Add the stock, bring to a boil, and simmer for 1 hour, skimming regularly.
6. Strain through a fine sieve and reduce further until it coats the back of a spoon.

## Secret Tips
- **The Pastry Chill:** Never skip the chilling steps. Cold pastry hitting a hot oven is what creates the steam for those flaky layers.
- **Duxelles Dryness:** If your mushrooms are still wet, they will steam the pastry from the inside, making it soggy.
- **The Ham Barrier:** The Parma ham acts as a moisture barrier between the mushrooms and the pastry.

## Origin
While named after the Duke of Wellington, the dish as we know it today was popularized in the mid-20th century. Gordon Ramsay's version at the **Savoy Grill** has become the definitive modern standard, known for its technical perfection and rich, concentrated flavors.
