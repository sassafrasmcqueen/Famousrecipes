# Junior's Famous New York Cheesecake

The "Original New York" cheesecake: rich, creamy, and velvety with its signature sponge cake crust.

### Ingredients
**The Sponge Cake Crust:**
- **All-Purpose Flour:** 65g (1/2 cup), sifted.
- **Baking Powder:** 1 tsp.
- **Salt:** A pinch.
- **Large Eggs:** 2 (separated).
- **Sugar:** 100g (1/2 cup), plus 2 tbsp for egg whites.
- **Vanilla Extract:** 1 tsp.
- **Lemon Extract:** 2 drops.
- **Unsalted Butter:** 30g (2 tbsp), melted and cooled.
- **Cream of Tartar:** 1/4 tsp.

**The Cheese Filling:**
- **Cream Cheese:** 900g (4x 8-oz packages), room temperature.
- **Sugar:** 330g (1 2/3 cups).
- **Cornstarch:** 30g (2 tbsp).
- **Vanilla Extract:** 1 tbsp.
- **Large Eggs:** 2.
- **Heavy Cream:** 180ml (3/4 cup).

### Instructions
1. **Prep the Crust:** Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Grease a 9-inch springform pan.
2. **Sponge Layer:** Beat egg yolks and 1/2 cup sugar until thick and lemon-colored. Stir in vanilla and lemon extracts. Sift flour, baking powder, and salt over the mixture; fold in by hand. Blend in melted butter.
3. **Meringue:** In a clean bowl, beat egg whites and cream of tartar until frothy. Gradually add 2 tbsp sugar and beat until stiff peaks form. Fold whites into the batter gently. 
4. **Bake Crust:** Spoon batter into the pan and bake for 10 minutes until the center springs back. Let cool in the pan (do not remove).
5. **The Filling:** Increase oven to 450°F (175°C) briefly then lower to 350°F (see step 7). Beat one 8-oz package of cream cheese, 1/3 cup sugar, and cornstarch on low for 3 minutes. Beat in remaining cream cheese.
6. **Finish Filling:** Increase speed and beat in remaining sugar and vanilla. Blend in eggs one at a time, then the heavy cream. Do not overmix.
7. **Water Bath:** Pour filling over the sponge crust. Place springform pan in a larger shallow pan with 1 inch of hot water.
8. **Bake:** Bake at 350°F (175°C) for about 1 hour until the center barely jiggles. 
9. **Cooling:** Cool on a wire rack for 1 hour, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours or overnight before removing from the pan.

### Secret Tips
- **Room Temperature is Law:** To avoid lumps, the cream cheese, eggs, and cream *must* be at room temperature.
- **The Sponge Crust:** Most NY cheesecakes use Graham crackers, but Junior's signature is the thin layer of sponge cake which absorbs some of the moisture from the filling.
- **Don't Overmix:** Overmixing the batter after adding eggs incorporates too much air, which causes the cheesecake to crack and puff up like a souffle.

### Origin Story
Harry Rosen opened Junior's on Flatbush Avenue in Brooklyn in 1950. He spent years working with his baker, Eigel Petersen, to perfect the "New York style" cheesecake. Today, it remains the gold standard for New York cheesecake, served to presidents, celebrities, and locals alike.
