# Nando's Style Peri-Peri Chicken

The soul of South African-Portuguese fusion. This recipe delivers the signature flame-grilled flavor and the addictive, tangy heat of the Bird's Eye chili (Peri-Peri).

## Ingredients

### The Chicken
- **1 Whole** Chicken (approx. 1.5kg), spatchcocked (backbone removed and flattened)
- **1 tsp** Coarse sea salt

### Homemade Peri-Peri Marinade (Medium Heat)
- **6-8** African Bird's Eye chilies (or Thai red chilies), roughly chopped
- **4 cloves** Garlic, peeled
- **1/2 cup** Fresh lemon juice
- **1/4 cup** Red wine vinegar
- **1/4 cup** Olive oil
- **1 tbsp** Smoked paprika
- **1 tbsp** Dried oregano
- **1 tsp** Onion powder
- **1 tsp** Salt
- **1/2 tsp** Black pepper

## Instructions

### 1. Prepare the Marinade
1. Place all marinade ingredients in a blender or food processor.
2. Pulse until relatively smooth but with some visible chili flakes remaining.
3. **Secret Tip:** Simmer half of the marinade in a small saucepan for 5 minutes. Use the raw half for marinating and the simmered half for basting during grilling. This creates layers of flavor.

### 2. Marinate the Chicken
1. Pat the spatchcocked chicken dry. Rub salt all over.
2. Place the chicken in a large resealable bag or shallow dish and pour over the "raw" half of the marinade.
3. Massage the marinade into the skin and under the skin of the breast and thighs.
4. Marinate in the refrigerator for **at least 24 hours**. This is non-negotiable for authentic Nando's depth.

### 3. Flame-Grilling
1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (aiming for around 200°C/400°F).
2. Place the chicken on the grill, skin-side down first.
3. Grill for 12-15 minutes, then flip.
4. Baste generously with the "simmered" basting sauce every 5 minutes.
5. Continue cooking until the internal temperature reaches 74°C (165°F) and the skin is charred and crispy in spots.

### 4. Resting
1. Remove from the grill and let the chicken rest for 10 minutes.
2. Brush with one final layer of basting sauce before serving.

## Secret Tips
- **The Spatchcock:** Flattening the chicken is essential for even cooking and maximum surface area for the marinade and char.
- **The Peri-Peri Chili:** Authentic Nando's uses the African Bird's Eye chili. If you can't find them, Thai red chilies are the best substitute, but add a pinch of lemon zest to mimic the citrusy notes of the Peri-Peri.
- **The Char:** Nando's is all about the "flame-grilled" taste. If using an oven, finish the chicken under the broiler for 3-4 minutes to get those blackened edges.

## Origin
**Nando's** was founded in 1987 in Rosettenville, Johannesburg, South Africa, by friends Robert Brozin and Fernando Duarte. They discovered a Portuguese eatery serving Peri-Peri chicken and decided to bring the flavor to the world. It is now a global staple of casual dining.
